How Roofing Customers Buy

Roofing is a high-ticket, trust-intensive purchase. The average residential roof replacement is $8,000-$15,000+. Understanding the customer journey is essential for marketing success.

Storm Damage / Insurance Claims (40-60% in storm-prone areas)

After hail or wind damage, homeowners need fast inspections and insurance help:

  • Search immediately after storms: "roof damage inspection [city]"
  • Want someone who works with insurance companies
  • Speed matters — roofers who respond fast get the job
  • Trust is critical (fear of storm chasers)

Planned Roof Replacement (40-60% of jobs)

Aging roofs that need replacement. These customers:

  • Research for weeks or months
  • Get 3-5 quotes (it's a big investment)
  • Check reviews extensively
  • Care about materials, warranties, and company reputation
  • Often ask neighbors for recommendations

Roofing has the highest customer lifetime value in home services. One roof job can be worth $10,000+, making each lead extremely valuable.

The Roofing Customer Journey

  1. Trigger: Storm damage, leak, or aging roof noticed
  2. Research: Google searches, asking neighbors, reading reviews
  3. Quote requests: Contact 3-5 companies for estimates
  4. Evaluation: Compare price, materials, warranties, professionalism
  5. Decision: Choose based on trust, value, and timing

Common Roofing Marketing Mistakes

Mistake #1: Chasing Storms Without Local Presence

"Storm chaser" roofers have given the industry a bad name. Homeowners are skeptical of out-of-town companies. Building a strong local presence with reviews and community involvement builds trust.

Mistake #2: No Portfolio of Completed Work

Roofing is visual. Customers want to see before/after photos, different materials, completed projects in their area. If your website and Google profile don't showcase your work, you're losing to competitors who do.

Mistake #3: Slow Response to Leads

Roofing customers often contact multiple companies. The first roofer to arrive for an inspection usually gets the job. Responding in hours instead of minutes costs you thousands.

Mistake #4: Not Capturing Insurance Work

Many roofers avoid insurance claims because they're complex. But insurance jobs often have guaranteed payment and repeat business (neighbors see your trucks and signs).

Mistake #5: Depending Only on Door Knocking

Door-to-door works but it's inefficient and increasingly unwelcome. Digital marketing reaches homeowners who are actively searching, not interrupting dinner.

What Actually Drives Roofing Leads

1. Google Local Services Ads

LSAs are essential for roofers. The "Google Guaranteed" badge builds trust in an industry plagued by scammers. Customers can see your reviews and licensing immediately.

  • Average cost per lead: $50-$100
  • Close rate: 15-25% (lower than other trades due to high ticket)
  • Revenue per closed deal: $8,000-$15,000+
  • ROI: 5-10x when properly managed

2. Google Ads for High-Intent Keywords

Target searches from ready-to-buy customers:

  • "Roof replacement [city]" — high intent, high value
  • "Roofing company near me" — broad but effective
  • "Storm damage roof repair" — urgent, insurance-ready
  • "Metal roof installation [city]" — premium customers

3. Review Generation Strategy

Reviews are more important for roofing than almost any other trade. Customers are spending $10,000+ and want proof you're legitimate.

  • Target: 100+ reviews on Google
  • Include photos in review requests
  • Respond to every review (positive and negative)
  • Showcase reviews on your website

4. Neighborhood Marketing

One roof job can generate 2-3 neighbor referrals. Maximize this with:

  • Yard signs during and after projects
  • Door hangers on the block
  • Ask for referrals explicitly
  • Offer referral bonuses ($200-$500)

Local vs National Roofing Marketing

Local Roofing Marketing (Your Focus)

  • Service area: 30-50 mile radius typical
  • Keywords: "Roofing company [city]," "roof replacement [area]"
  • Competition: 20-100+ roofers per market
  • Strategy: LSAs, Google Maps, local SEO, neighborhood presence
  • Budget: $2,000-$6,000/month competitive in most markets

National Roofing Brands

  • Examples: Roof Maxx, CertainTeed contractors
  • Strategy: Brand partnerships, certification programs
  • Reality: Most roofing is local; national brands matter less than local reputation

Roofing is intensely local. Homeowners hire roofers they trust, and trust is built through local reputation, reviews, and community presence.

Example Roofing Growth Strategy

Here's a 6-month roadmap for a roofing company doing $800K/year wanting to reach $1.2M:

Month 1-2: Foundation

  • Optimize Google Business Profile with photos, services, reviews
  • Launch Google Local Services Ads ($1,500/month)
  • Implement instant lead response (text + email + call)
  • Set up systematic review requests
  • Target: 20-30 leads/month

Month 3-4: Scale

  • Add Google Ads for high-value keywords ($2,000/month)
  • Build service and area pages on website
  • Launch referral program ($300 per closed referral)
  • Target: 40-50 leads/month

Month 5-6: Optimize

  • Analyze which lead sources have best close rates
  • Add retargeting for website visitors
  • Begin content marketing for SEO
  • Expand geographic targeting based on results
  • Target: 60-75 leads/month

Expected results: 60-75 leads/month at $75-$100 per lead. At 20% close rate and $10,000 average job, that's $120,000-$150,000 in new monthly revenue.
