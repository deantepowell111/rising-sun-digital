How Electrical Customers Buy

Electrical work sits between emergency and planned services. Understanding when and how customers search helps you capture more leads.

Emergency Electrical (40-50% of residential calls)

Power outages, sparking outlets, tripped breakers that won't reset. These customers:

  • Search immediately on their phone
  • Look for "24 hour electrician" or "emergency electrician near me"
  • Call the first licensed, reviewed company they find
  • Will pay premium rates for immediate service

Planned Electrical Work (50-60% of calls)

Panel upgrades, EV charger installations, rewiring, lighting projects. These customers:

  • Research for days or weeks
  • Get 2-4 quotes before deciding
  • Value expertise, licensing, and reviews
  • Often come from contractor referrals (new construction, remodels)

Electricians have a unique advantage: customers MUST hire a licensed professional. This reduces DIY competition and increases trust in credentialed businesses.

The Electrical Customer Journey

  1. Need identified: Electrical problem or project requirement
  2. Search: Google "electrician near me" or specific service
  3. Evaluate: Check licensing, reviews, Google Guaranteed badge
  4. Contact: Call or request quote from 1-3 companies
  5. Decide: Based on professionalism, price, availability

Common Electrician Marketing Mistakes

Mistake #1: Relying Solely on Contractor Referrals

Referrals from general contractors and builders are great, but they're unpredictable and often come with slim margins. Residential customers found through your own marketing typically pay higher rates.

Mistake #2: No Online Presence Beyond a Basic Website

Many electricians have a website but no Google Business Profile, no reviews, no LSAs. When homeowners search "electrician near me," these businesses are invisible.

Mistake #3: Not Showcasing Licensing & Credentials

Electrical work is dangerous. Customers want proof you're qualified. If your license number, insurance, and certifications aren't prominently displayed, you're losing trust.

Mistake #4: Ignoring Emerging Markets

EV charger installation is exploding. Solar connections are growing. Smart home electrical is mainstream. Electricians not marketing these services are missing high-value leads.

Mistake #5: DIY Marketing That Wastes Time

Electricians are skilled tradespeople. Spending hours on marketing takes away from billable work. If marketing costs you 10 hours/month at $75/hour, that's $750 in lost revenue — more than hiring help.

What Actually Drives Electrician Leads

1. Google Local Services Ads

LSAs are perfect for electricians because the "Google Guaranteed" badge builds instant trust. Customers know Google has verified your license and insurance.

  • Average cost per lead: $30-$60
  • Close rate: 25-35%
  • Best services: Emergency calls, panel upgrades, EV chargers

2. Google Business Profile

Free leads from the Map Pack. Critical elements for electricians:

  • License number displayed prominently
  • Photos of completed projects (before/after)
  • 50+ reviews with electrical-specific keywords
  • All services listed with descriptions
  • Service area clearly defined

3. Google Ads for High-Value Services

Target keywords that attract profitable jobs:

  • "EV charger installation [city]" — $800-$2,000+ jobs
  • "Electrical panel upgrade" — $1,500-$3,000+ jobs
  • "Emergency electrician near me" — premium rates
  • "Licensed electrician [city]" — trust signal keywords

4. Service-Specific Landing Pages

Create pages targeting specific high-value services:

  • EV charger installation page
  • Electrical panel upgrade page
  • Whole-home generator installation page
  • Smart home wiring page

Local vs National Electrician Marketing

Local Electrical Marketing (Your Focus)

  • Service area: 20-40 mile radius typical
  • Keywords: "[Service] electrician [city]"
  • Competition: 15-75 licensed electricians per market
  • Strategy: Dominate LSAs, Google Maps, local search
  • Budget: $1,000-$3,000/month competitive in most areas

National Electrical Brands

  • Examples: Mister Sparky, Mr. Electric
  • Advantage: Brand recognition, national marketing support
  • Disadvantage: Franchise fees, less flexibility
  • Reality: Local electricians can outrank franchises with good marketing

Licensing requirements limit competition. Unlike other trades, not just anyone can call themselves an electrician. This makes marketing more effective per dollar spent.

Example Electrician Growth Strategy

Here's a 6-month roadmap for an electrical company doing $350K/year wanting to reach $500K:

Month 1-2: Foundation

  • Optimize Google Business Profile with license, photos, services
  • Launch Google Local Services Ads ($1,000/month)
  • Set up call tracking and lead attribution
  • Implement automated review requests
  • Target: 12-18 leads/month

Month 3-4: Expand

  • Add Google Ads for high-value services ($800/month)
  • Create service-specific landing pages (EV chargers, panels)
  • Build out service area pages
  • Target: 25-35 leads/month

Month 5-6: Optimize

  • Analyze which services have best margins, focus there
  • Launch retargeting campaigns
  • Begin SEO for emerging services (EV, solar, smart home)
  • Target: 35-45 leads/month

Expected results: 35-45 leads/month at $45-$65 per lead. At 30% close rate and $650 average ticket, that's $6,800-$8,700 in new monthly revenue.
